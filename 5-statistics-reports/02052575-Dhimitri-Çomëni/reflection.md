# Personal Reflection

While researching AI statistics and industry reports, I realized how quickly artificial intelligence is changing the software engineering profession. Before conducting this research, I knew AI was becoming popular, but I did not fully understand how widely it is already being used by developers and companies.

The most surprising finding was the level of investment that organizations are making in AI technologies. Major technology companies are investing billions of dollars into AI research and development, which shows that AI will continue playing an important role in the future of software engineering.

I was also interested in the results of developer surveys showing that many programmers already use AI tools in their daily work. These tools can improve productivity and help developers complete tasks more efficiently. However, I also learned that AI-generated code is not always correct and still requires careful review and testing.

One concern I have is that some developers may become too dependent on AI tools. If programmers stop practicing problem-solving and critical thinking, their technical skills could weaken over time. For this reason, I believe AI should be used as an assistant rather than a replacement for human knowledge.

This research changed my perspective on the future of software engineering. Instead of viewing AI as a threat, I now see it as a tool that can help developers become more productive when used responsibly. In the future, I plan to continue learning about AI technologies while also strengthening my programming, software design, and analytical skills. I believe that software engineers who combine technical expertise with the ability to work effectively alongside AI will remain valuable throughout their careers.
